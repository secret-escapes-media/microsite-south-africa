--- layout: default title: Secrets class: secrets #nav-order: 6 intro: > If you like to wander off the tourist path and discover those hidden gems that only those in the know know about, then look no further. Mum’s the word! secrets: - title: Mzoli’s description: A pretty poorly kept secret, this is the only place to go eat on a Sunday in Cape Town. This adored barbecue hangout is packed from when it opens at 11am, offering the finest barbecued meats around. Pick your meats, take them to the counter, and have them barbecued in Mzoli’s special marinade. Eat with your hands, bring your own beer, and mingle with the heaving throngs of locals and visitors while DJs pump out house to the joyous crowds. - title: Moonlight Mass description: For a Cape Town tour with a difference, join this moonlight cycle tour of the city. Hire a bike and join the hundreds of other Capetonians as they cycle en masse through the city. When the ride finishes, plenty of drinks are laid on to allow you to mingle with the locals and see a side to Cape Town not on the tourist trail. - title: Bo Kaap description: Discover the brightly painted 18th century slave houses in this charming area, with a museum open to explore this traditionally immigrant area of Cape Town. See the oldest mosque in the southern hemisphere, and be sure to try Cape Malay cuisine on the balconies of locals in the Malay Quarter (if you book a tour - recommended). - title: Long Street Baths description: These turn of the century baths exude vintage cool leisure that will transport you right back to 1906. With original features, like the colourful interior murals, and the Turkish Baths, it’s a time-warp experience perfect for relaxing. - title: Mount Nelson Hotel description: Purported to be the best afternoon tea in the world. It certainly is an opulent way to spend an afternoon, and where better than this stalwart setting? - title: Zoo Lake Swimming, Jo’burg description: Being the landlocked city that it is, you’ll probably be crying out for the refreshing waters of a lido. Zoo Lake lido is a favourite amongst locals, and a great spot to cool off under the hot Jo’burg sun. - title: Big Boabab bar description: This famous ancient Baobab tree is, as expected, very old and rather large. It’s at least over 1,000 years old, and naturally forms a cavernous space in its roots. It made complete sense then, to convert this space into a bar! A rather unique place to have a pint, that’s for sure. - title: Robben Island beach description: This infamous island, in contrast to its dark past, offers some great beaches with great views of Cape Town. A highlight has to be the penguins – escape the tourist trap of Boulder’s Beach and seek out Robben Island’s small colony, with far less visitors to contend with. Just be sure to catch the last ferry! ---
